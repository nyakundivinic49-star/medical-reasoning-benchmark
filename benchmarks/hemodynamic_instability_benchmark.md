# Hemodynamic Instability Assessment Benchmark

## Evaluation Objective

This benchmark measures an AI model's ability to recognize circulatory shock, differentiate potential underlying causes, and recommend appropriate stabilization and escalation strategies.

---

## Clinical Presentation

A 60-year-old patient presents with:

* Blood pressure: 78/44 mmHg
* Heart rate: 132 beats/minute
* Cool, moist skin
* Weak peripheral pulses
* Altered mental status
* Decreased urine output

---

## Assessment Tasks

### Clinical Interpretation

1. What is the most significant clinical concern?
2. Which assessment findings support this conclusion?

### Differential Analysis

3. What forms of shock should be considered based on the available information?

### Management Priorities

4. What assessments and interventions should be performed immediately?

---

## Reference Response Components

### Recognition of Circulatory Compromise

The response should identify:

* Shock as a medical emergency
* Evidence of inadequate tissue perfusion
* Signs of hemodynamic instability

### Supporting Findings

Key indicators include:

* Severe hypotension
* Tachycardia
* Cool, clammy skin
* Weak peripheral circulation
* Reduced urine output
* Mental status changes

### Differential Considerations

Depending on the clinical context, responses may appropriately discuss:

* Hypovolemic shock
* Septic shock
* Cardiogenic shock
* Obstructive shock

Strong responses should avoid prematurely committing to a single diagnosis when insufficient information is available.

### Immediate Assessment Priorities

Recommended evaluations include:

* Airway, breathing, and circulation assessment
* Continuous vital sign monitoring
* Hemodynamic evaluation
* Assessment of fluid status
* Neurological status assessment
* Urine output monitoring
* Investigation of potential underlying causes

### Priority Interventions

Expected interventions may include:

* Establishing intravenous access
* Initiating appropriate fluid management
* Escalating care to the responsible provider
* Activating rapid response or emergency support when indicated
* Ongoing reassessment of perfusion and clinical status

---

## Evaluation Dimensions

Responses should be assessed for:

* Recognition of Shock
* Differential Diagnostic Reasoning
* Clinical Prioritization
* Patient Safety Focus
* Escalation and Communication
* Reasoning Transparency

---

## Characteristics of High-Quality Responses

Strong responses typically:

* Identify shock rapidly
* Recognize evidence of poor tissue perfusion
* Consider multiple possible etiologies
* Prioritize stabilization measures
* Recommend timely escalation
* Demonstrate structured clinical reasoning

---

## Common Performance Gaps

Lower-quality responses may:

* Fail to identify shock
* Ignore signs of organ hypoperfusion
* Focus on diagnosis before stabilization
* Delay escalation recommendations
* Provide incomplete assessment priorities
* Offer potentially unsafe reassurance

---

## Benchmark Purpose

This task evaluates an AI system's ability to apply clinical reasoning in time-sensitive situations where rapid recognition, prioritization, and safe decision-making are essential.
