# Septic Shock Recognition Assessment

## Benchmark Goal

This evaluation task measures an AI model's ability to identify sepsis-related deterioration, recognize signs of septic shock, and recommend timely, safety-focused clinical interventions.

---

## Patient Scenario

A 72-year-old patient presents with the following findings:

* Temperature: 39.1°C
* Heart rate: 125 beats/minute
* Blood pressure: 82/48 mmHg
* Respiratory rate: 30 breaths/minute
* Elevated serum lactate
* Decreased urine output
* Altered mental status

---

## Evaluation Questions

### Clinical Assessment

1. What condition should be considered the highest priority concern?
2. Which assessment findings support this conclusion?

### Management Priorities

3. What interventions should be initiated immediately?
4. What adverse outcomes may develop if treatment is delayed?

---

## Reference Response Components

High-quality responses should include:

### Recognition of Critical Illness

* Identification of probable septic shock
* Recognition of systemic infection with circulatory compromise
* Identification of organ dysfunction indicators

### Supporting Clinical Findings

* Hypotension
* Tachycardia
* Fever
* Increased respiratory rate
* Elevated lactate levels
* Reduced urine output
* Mental status changes

### Immediate Management Priorities

* Airway, breathing, and circulation assessment
* Establishment of intravenous access
* Early fluid resuscitation
* Blood culture collection when feasible before antimicrobial therapy
* Prompt administration of broad-spectrum antibiotics
* Ongoing monitoring of lactate trends
* Close urine output monitoring
* Immediate escalation to the appropriate medical or critical care team

### Potential Consequences of Delayed Treatment

* Progressive circulatory failure
* Multi-organ dysfunction
* Respiratory failure
* Acute kidney injury
* Increased mortality risk

---

## Assessment Categories

Responses should be evaluated using the following dimensions:

* Medical Correctness
* Recognition of Clinical Deterioration
* Intervention Prioritization
* Patient Safety Awareness
* Clinical Reasoning and Justification
* Escalation Appropriateness

---

## Indicators of a High-Performing Response

A strong response should:

* Correctly identify septic shock
* Explain why the findings are concerning
* Prioritize urgent interventions
* Emphasize timely escalation
* Demonstrate clear clinical reasoning
* Avoid unsupported assumptions or unsafe recommendations

---

## Common Failure Patterns

Lower-quality responses may:

* Fail to recognize septic shock
* Underestimate clinical urgency
* Delay antibiotic or fluid recommendations
* Omit escalation guidance
* Ignore signs of organ dysfunction
* Provide incomplete or unsafe management advice
