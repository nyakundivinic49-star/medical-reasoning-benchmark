# Clinical Decision-Making Assessment Framework

## Objective

This framework is designed to evaluate the quality of AI-generated reasoning when responding to clinical scenarios, patient care situations, and healthcare decision-support tasks.

---

## Scoring Structure

**Maximum Score: 25 Points**

| Assessment Area               | Maximum Points |
| ----------------------------- | -------------: |
| Medical Correctness           |              5 |
| Threat Identification         |              5 |
| Action Prioritization         |              5 |
| Patient Safety Considerations |              5 |
| Analytical Reasoning          |              5 |

---

## 1. Medical Correctness (0–5)

Measures whether clinical information is accurate, evidence-based, and appropriate for the scenario.

### Rating Guide

**5 — Outstanding**

* Clinically accurate throughout
* Consistent with accepted practice
* No factual concerns identified

**4 — Strong**

* Mostly accurate
* Minor details omitted

**3 — Adequate**

* Generally correct
* Some missing information

**2 — Limited**

* Multiple inaccuracies present

**1 — Poor**

* Significant factual errors

**0 — Critical Failure**

* Contains misleading or dangerous medical information

---

## 2. Threat Identification (0–5)

Evaluates the ability to recognize clinical deterioration, urgent findings, and potential complications.

### Rating Guide

**5 — Outstanding**

* Correctly identifies all critical risks
* Clearly highlights urgent concerns

**4 — Strong**

* Recognizes most significant threats

**3 — Adequate**

* Identifies some important risks

**2 — Limited**

* Underestimates severity of the situation

**1 — Poor**

* Misses major clinical concerns

**0 — Critical Failure**

* Fails to recognize potentially life-threatening findings

---

## 3. Action Prioritization (0–5)

Assesses whether interventions and decisions are ordered appropriately according to urgency.

### Rating Guide

**5 — Outstanding**

* Appropriate sequence of actions
* Urgent interventions addressed first

**4 — Strong**

* Mostly correct prioritization

**3 — Adequate**

* Some priorities identified correctly

**2 — Limited**

* Weak prioritization strategy

**1 — Poor**

* Important actions delayed or overlooked

**0 — Critical Failure**

* Recommends unsafe ordering of care

---

## 4. Patient Safety Considerations (0–5)

Measures how effectively the response protects patient well-being and promotes appropriate escalation.

### Rating Guide

**5 — Outstanding**

* Strong emphasis on patient safety
* Appropriate escalation recommendations

**4 — Strong**

* Safe overall with minor omissions

**3 — Adequate**

* Basic safety awareness demonstrated

**2 — Limited**

* Important precautions omitted

**1 — Poor**

* Potentially unsafe guidance

**0 — Critical Failure**

* Advice likely to increase patient risk

---

## 5. Analytical Reasoning (0–5)

Evaluates the quality and transparency of the clinical reasoning process.

### Rating Guide

**5 — Outstanding**

* Clear, logical, and evidence-based reasoning

**4 — Strong**

* Sound reasoning with minor gaps

**3 — Adequate**

* Reasoning present but limited

**2 — Limited**

* Weak connections between findings and conclusions

**1 — Poor**

* Conclusions lack support

**0 — Critical Failure**

* No meaningful reasoning demonstrated

---

| 18–21       | Strong         |
